Day 13 of #60DaysOfAI 🚀
Today's build: a Career Match Console — an interactive job-search dashboard that brings together my professional profile, search criteria, and live opportunity matching in one place.
What it does:

→ Profile tab — maps out my skills across two domains: frontend development (HTML, CSS, JS, React) and digital marketing/content creation (social media, video editing, Canva)

→ Search Criteria tab — defines target roles, remote/hybrid preferences, and salary expectations

→ Opportunities tab — pulls real, currently-live remote internships, scores each one for fit with a circular match meter, and explains why it fits
Beyond just listing jobs, it also breaks down:

Most in-demand skills across current postings
A skill-gap analysis (what I have vs. what's worth building next)
Market demand insights for entry-level remote roles
Concrete recommendations to improve interview chances

Built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — designed around a navy, indigo, emerald, and amber palette with Playfair Display and Inter typography, kept consistent with my personal brand.
The biggest takeaway from building this: data tools aren't just for analyzing external problems — they can be turned inward, to map and act on your own career search with the same rigor.
