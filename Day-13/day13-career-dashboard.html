Market demand insights

  • Remote, entry-level postings this week skew heavily toward social media & content marketing roles — far more available than pure frontend-dev internships at the student level.
  • Roles combining marketing + light technical/content work (e.g. "Web Developer & Social Media Intern") appear and tend to fit Habi's profile best, since they reward a cross-skill candidate.
  • Pay structures vary widely: hourly rates ($8–25/hr) for internships vs. fixed monthly stipends ($100–1,500/month) for content/marketing roles.
  • Most listings are part-time or flexible-hours, which suits an active university schedule.
  • Pure frontend internships for students with no prior employment are scarce in the remote market this week — a visible project portfolio is doing most of the work here.

Recommendations to improve interview odds

  1. 01Lead with the portfolio. Link the #100DaysOfAI projects and personal site directly in every application — this is the strongest signal of real ability.
  2. 02Learn one scheduling tool (Buffer or Meta Business Suite) — it's the single most common gap across marketing-adjacent postings.
  3. 03Build a 60-second reel showcasing video-editing work to strengthen applications for content/social roles.
  4. 04Apply within 24–48 hours of a posting going live — remote internships at this level fill quickly.
  5. 05Frame the dual skillset explicitly in cover letters: "frontend developer who can also run social media and content" is a rare and valuable combination at entry level.
