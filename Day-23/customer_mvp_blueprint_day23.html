Executive Summary

Overview

GigBridge PK is a student-first micro-gig marketplace designed specifically for Pakistan's 1.2 million tech and CS students. The platform solves a fundamental catch-22: students need client experience to get hired, but platforms like Fiverr require reviews before clients trust new sellers. GigBridge breaks this deadlock with a mentored onboarding system, local payment rails, and hyperlocal matching.

The customer is crystal clear — a 19–24 year old CS/IT student in KPK or Punjab, bilingual (Urdu/Pashto + English), smartphone-first, and deeply motivated to earn before graduating. On the demand side, Pakistan's 3.5 million small businesses are hungry for affordable digital help but distrust unaccountable global freelancers.

The MVP is straightforward: a lightweight marketplace with student profiles, gig listings, in-app messaging, JazzCash/EasyPaisa payment escrow, and a review system. Build this in 4 weeks. Validate with 50 student sellers and 20 business clients. The goal: 10 completed, paid gigs with a 4+ star average in 30 days.

Top 10 Customer Pain Points

1
Zero-review catch-22
Can't get clients without reviews; can't get reviews without clients. The biggest platform barrier for students.
CRITICAL
2
No local payment withdrawal
Payoneer requires bank accounts & ID students don't have. JazzCash and EasyPaisa are not supported on global platforms.
CRITICAL
3
Client scams & non-payment
Students do free "test" work and never get paid. No escrow protection on informal WhatsApp gigs.
CRITICAL
4
English-only platforms
Urdu and Pashto-speaking students struggle to navigate, write proposals, and communicate on global platforms.
HIGH
5
No pricing guidance
First-timers under-price by 70%+ or scare clients with over-priced quotes. No local benchmarks exist.
HIGH
6
Portfolio creation barrier
Students have coursework but no client projects. Platforms require a polished portfolio to attract attention.
HIGH
7
Buyers can't trust student quality
Local SMBs want affordable help but fear wasted money on unverified students. Trust deficit on both sides.
HIGH
8
No micro-gig category ($5–$30)
Global platforms push $50+ minimums. Pakistani micro-businesses need $5–$30 tasks with fast turnaround.
MEDIUM
9
Parental skepticism of freelancing
Many students need family buy-in to spend study time on gigs. No credibility narrative for parents.
MEDIUM
10
No mentorship or skill verification
Students don't know if their skills are "good enough." No trusted local body certifies student freelance readiness.
MEDIUM

Pricing Hypothesis

Micro Gig
PKR 500–2,000
~$2–$7 USD · Quick tasks, under 24h
Social media post (1–3 posts)
Logo concept (1 revision)
Data entry / spreadsheet task
Simple banner design
Platform commission: 10%
Standard Gig
PKR 3,000–10,000
~$10–$36 · Core sweet spot
Landing page (HTML/CSS)
Social media package (1 week)
Content writing (5 articles)
App UI mockup (3 screens)
Platform commission: 12%
Pro Project
PKR 15,000–50,000
~$54–$180 · Milestone-based
Full website (5+ pages)
Monthly social media management
Mobile app prototype
Long-form SEO content series
Platform commission: 10%
💡 Pricing Insight: Start with the Micro & Standard tiers only. This lowers buyer risk and builds the review base rapidly. Unlock Pro Projects once seller has 5+ reviews. Platform earns a blended ~11% commission — target PKR 2,000/month per active seller at 30-day launch.

Full-time student + internship + 60-day challenge = overload		 Medium Medium Use no-code tools (Glide, Bubble) for MVP. Recruit 1–2 CS classmates as co-founders or volunteer moderators.
