Day 28/60 — Built a Hospital Admission Readiness Simulator 🏥
Today's project dives into one of healthcare's most complex workflows — getting a patient from "needs admission" to "admitted" without things falling through the cracks.
The simulator puts you in the role of a Hospital Admission Coordinator. You configure the diagnosis, admission type, and PA status — then navigate the chaos.
What's inside:

→ Prior Authorization branches (Approved / Pending / Denied + Appeal flow)

→ Weighted readiness score across 6 clinical & admin dimensions

→ CMS 2-Midnight Rule alert for Observation admissions

→ InterQual/Milliman criteria flags for Acute MI & CHF

→ Risk tracking across Documentation, Insurance, Bed & Clinical

→ Governance Snapshot with industry benchmarks at 75%+

→ Final admit/not-ready decision at the 90% threshold
Built with Claude AI — single-file HTML, zero dependencies.
Healthcare coordination is invisible until it breaks. This simulator makes the logic visible.
